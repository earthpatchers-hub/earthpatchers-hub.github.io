# Module 1 — Missions HQ (MVP)
## Short version
Missions HQ is where EarthPatchers begins. It’s a library of real-life missions for kids, teens,
and adults, designed to make “saving the Earth” feel doable.
It supports different lives:
- tiny missions for busy families (10–20 minutes)
- weekend missions for deeper impact (1–2 hours)
- team missions for schools, youth groups, and community teams
For adults, missions can be matched to skills (repairing, organising, teaching, gardening,
design, photography) and time available, which helps volunteering feel efficient and rewarding.
For kids, missions feel like adventures.
This structure also makes projects easier to repeat across communities: once a mission works
well, it can be reused by other groups without starting from scratch.
Rewards stay simple:
- Patch Coins are earned through missions and learning and can be used in the Market Hut
(and later Games).
- Badges are about who you are becoming (brave, kind, curious).
- Achievements are things you did, like stickers in your book.

# 2) Tribes (MVP)
## Short version
A Tribe is EarthPatchers’ word for a small team (like a club). Tribes can be:
- Kids/Teens Tribes (aliases + avatars, no visible kids’ faces, kid activity visible only in the Tribe)
- Adults-only Tribes (adults choose their visibility)
Missions can be shared across Tribes so great missions spread without exposing children’s
accounts.
Tribe “camp rules” keep things kind and clean:
- Be kind
- Keep private details private
- No visible kids’ faces
- Don’t post littering (and don’t encourage it)
- Use a Littering report when something shouldn’t be there
### Next steps (inside Tribes)
- More Tribe types: schools, neighbourhoods, NGOs, interest-based Tribes
- Better mission sharing (“Mission Shelf” search + favourites)
- Optional area-based Tribe challenges (monthly/seasonal, not overwhelming)
- Cleaner tools for moderation and “littering reports”
- Optional adult-only community events and learning circles

# 3) Market Hut (Safe Circular Exchange) (MVP)
## Short version
new.
The Market Hut is a Cluj-first safe circular exchange where people reuse items instead of buying
Patch Coins make it work without awkward item-for-item matching: you earn coins by helping,
then use coins to choose items.
It can support swaps, donations, and fair “value learning” through Patch Coins (and later polite
bidding if desired).
Safety guidance is built into the flow. Adults choose their visibility, but children’s faces are never
allowed.
### Next steps (inside Market Hut)
- Optional polite bidding using Patch Coins for high-demand items (simple and kid-safe)
- Borrowing shelves (tools, books, sports gear, kids gear)
- Better trust signals (verified users, mission-verified trading privileges)
- Safer meetup/pickup guidance and clearer rules

# 4) Earth’s Side of the Story (Cluj-first, built step-by-step) (MVP)
## Short version
Earth’s Side of the Story is the learning-and-action world that starts in Cluj and connects
learning to missions.
It has three doors:
1) My Hazards
- Explains hazards in a friendly way:
- natural hazards
- natural hazards made worse by pollution/changes people cause
- hazards caused directly by people
- Short learning cards and quizzes earn Patch Coins.
2) Contamination & Recovery Zone (Cluj map)
Adults (and older teens where appropriate) can add a pin (photo + short note) for:
- littering hot spots
- damaged areas / broken things
- trees cut where they shouldn’t be
- places needing care

--- PAGE 16 ---

Pins can become missions (or requests for missions).
Later, this map can include verified helper pins:
- Helpers (NGOs, researchers, community groups)
- Missions (ways to help)
- Reports (things needing attention)
3) Learn & Play
Short learning paths and quizzes that connect back to missions and Patch Coins.
### Next steps (inside Earth’s Side of the Story)
- Tribe alerts: new pins can gently alert nearby Tribes so local teams can choose to help.
- New cities/countries open slowly via a Discovery Phase (data + partners + priority missions). If
discovery isn’t supported, the area stays “sleeping” until the community helps find data sources
or partners.
- Community help for discovery (finding data sources, mapping local helpers)
- Verified helper pins (NGOs/scientists/community groups) and mission pins
- Better learning paths (age-appropriate, adult paths too)
- Tribe alerts for new local pins (gentle and local-only)
