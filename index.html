EarthPatchers

Community-Powered Sustainability

EarthPatchers is a community-driven platform that helps families, young people, and local communities turn sustainability into everyday action. Through missions, safe collaboration, and circular habits, we make it easy to start small, act together, and grow real impact, starting in Cluj.

Explore the MVP Plan Browse Modules

Why EarthPatchers Exists

Most people want to help the planet, but life gets in the way. Sustainability can feel like too much research, too many options, too much guilt, and not enough clarity on what actually makes a difference. Young people feel climate anxiety, parents feel time pressure, and communities don't have a simple way to act together.

EarthPatchers exists because the missing piece isn't "more awareness." It's a clear, easy path from intention to action, with built-in trust, support, and a community that moves together.

What Makes EarthPatchers Different

🎯

Action-First

We don't start with lectures or reports. We start with small, realistic actions that fit into everyday life. One mission at a time.

👪

Designed for Families & Youth

Young people care deeply about the future. They deserve safe spaces, clear boundaries, meaningful progress, and adults who support them.

♻️

Everyday Practicality

Circular habits (swap, reuse, repair) are powerful but only work when they're easy and safe to do locally. We make that possible.

🌎

Local First, Then Scale

We start with one city (Cluj), build something that truly works, and expand with the community's support and trust.

MVP — 36-Month Plan

EarthPatchers is starting with a focused MVP that proves the platform works in real life, in Cluj, before we expand. This MVP has three pillars:

🎯

Missions

Real-world eco actions

Quick missions (10–20 min) and weekend missions (1–2 hours), filtered by age, interest, and time. Progress tracked with badges, points, and a personal timeline.

Missions turn climate anxiety into doable action and give families and youth a clear starting point.

🤝

Youth Community Pilot

Small, safe, meaningful

Small pilot groups in Cluj with a realistic rhythm: monthly or seasonal challenges. Clear rules, reporting, and adult/guardian involvement where needed.

Young people care deeply about their future. This gives them a safe way to contribute without overwhelm.

♻️

Safe Circular Exchange

Swap, give away, reuse

Local swap and give-away listings for Cluj. Safety-by-design: clear exchange rules, safe pickup practices, and moderation for suspicious behavior.

Circular habits are one of the fastest ways to reduce waste, but only work if people trust the system.

36-Month Timeline

Phase 1 · Months 0–12

Prove the Core Loop in Cluj

  • Launch the missions library and start tracking progress
  • Run the first youth community pilot groups with strong safety rules
  • Launch the circular exchange pilot with moderation and safe pickup guidance
  • Add the first "learning corner" (small, curated, age-aware)
Goal

Prove people return, participate, and feel safe.

Phase 2 · Months 13–24

Make It Stronger & Easier to Run

  • Improve mission quality and "mission packs" (seasonal collections)
  • Expand youth pilot groups if Phase 1 works well
  • Improve safe exchange flows (categories, templates, trust signals)
  • Grow the learning layer carefully (still staged)
Goal

Reduce admin/moderation effort while improving experience and safety.

Phase 3 · Months 25–36

Make It Stable & Scale-Ready

  • Make the three pillars robust enough for growth
  • Ensure safety workflows work at larger scale
  • Strengthen the learning layer into clear learning paths connected to missions
Goal

EarthPatchers becomes repeatable as a model, starting from Cluj.

The "Globe" Is Staged

Our future learning experience (an "Encarta-like" Earth globe/map) is added step-by-step as a learning layer that supports Missions:

  • Phase 1: A small learning corner (simple, clear, age-aware)
  • Phase 2: A richer learning map with carefully chosen topics
  • Phase 3: More complete learning paths (still focused on clarity and reliability)

We want learning to be trustworthy and understandable, not flashy and confusing.

What the MVP Does NOT Include

EarthPatchers has many future ideas (eco shops directory, idea backlog, games, city expansion tools). These are not part of the MVP and will be developed as Next Steps, so people don't confuse the MVP with everything we might build.

What Success Looks Like

  • ✅ Missions are completed and people return for more
  • 🔐 Youth participation is safe, supportive, and meaningful
  • ♻️ Circular exchange works smoothly with trust and moderation
  • 📚 The staged learning layer helps people understand "why" and "what to do next"
  • 🏙️ Cluj becomes a strong foundation for future expansion

In MVP

Missions HQ

The Heart of EarthPatchers

Missions HQ is the beating heart of EarthPatchers. It's where families and young people find real-world eco missions, from quick 10-minute actions to weekend adventures. Every mission is designed to be doable, age-appropriate, and meaningful. You can filter by time, interest, and age group, track your progress with badges and points, and build a personal timeline of everything you've done for the planet.

Why It Matters

Missions turn climate anxiety into doable action. They give families and young people a clear starting point, with a habit-first approach: one mission at a time, building real change step by step.

Loopy, the little bear, scratched his head and looked around the forest clearing. "What can I do today?" he wondered. The Missions HQ board was glowing softly under the big oak tree. "Plant a seed," one card said. "Pick up 5 pieces of litter near the stream," said another. "Help a neighbour carry their recycling." Loopy smiled. He didn't need to save the whole forest today. He just needed to start. He picked the seed card, tucked it into his bandana, and trotted off toward the meadow. One small mission, one small bear, one big difference.

Tribes

Your Local Crew

Tribes are small, local groups that do missions together, meet up, and support each other. Think of them as your neighbourhood crew for sustainability. Whether it's a school class, a group of parents, or a youth club, Tribes make EarthPatchers social, consistent, and fun. The rhythm is realistic, monthly or seasonal, not a weekly obligation. And every tribe is connected to the platform's safety system from day one.

Why It Matters

Sustainability isn't just about tasks. It's about belonging. Tribes turn the platform into a community where people support each other and act together.

Loopy arrived at the oak stump circle just as the sun was setting. The other animals were already there: Mira the fox, Pip the hedgehog, and old Barbu the badger. "Welcome to the Moonlight Tribe," Mira said. "We meet once a month, right here." "What do we do?" Loopy asked. "Whatever the forest needs," Barbu said. "Last month we fixed the trail. This month, we're planting wildflowers by the stream." "And next month?" "We'll decide together." Loopy sat down and smiled. He wasn't alone anymore.

Market Hut

Swap, Share, Reuse

Market Hut is where circular habits come to life. It's a safe, community-driven space for swapping, giving away, and reusing things locally. From kids' clothes and books to tools and toys, Market Hut makes it easy to pass things on instead of throwing them away. Everything is moderated, with clear exchange rules, adult-managed interactions, and safe pickup guidance. In the future, it will also include a "borrow library" for things you only need once.

Why It Matters

Circular habits are one of the fastest ways to reduce waste. Market Hut makes reuse safe, easy, and social, connecting it directly to missions so it becomes a habit, not a one-time act.

Loopy had outgrown his favourite rain boots. They were still good, just too small. He carried them to the Market Hut, a cosy wooden stall under the big chestnut tree. "I'd like to give these away," he said. Old Nana Owl checked the boots, nodded, and placed them on the shelf. "Someone will be very happy," she said. The next morning, Pip the hedgehog was splashing through puddles in bright green boots, grinning from ear to ear. "They fit!" he squeaked. Loopy waved. That was the best part: nothing wasted, everything shared.

Earth's Side of the Story

Learning That Grows with You

Earth's Side of the Story is EarthPatchers' learning module. It's an interactive, age-appropriate experience that helps people understand why sustainability matters, without overwhelm or guilt. Think of it like an "Encarta-style" globe and map, where you can explore topics like pollution, climate change, water, energy, and biodiversity, all explained in plain language, adapted for different age groups (5-7, 8-11, 12-14, 14-18). Learning paths include quizzes and mini-games that unlock new regions, badges, and skills. It's built to grow step by step, starting small and adding depth over time.

Why It Matters

Understanding "why" is the bridge between action and lasting change. This module makes climate science accessible, emotionally safe, and deeply connected to the missions people do in real life.

Loopy found the old globe in the library hollow. When he touched it, it glowed. "Where would you like to go?" it asked. "The ocean," Loopy whispered. The globe spun, and suddenly he was watching tiny fish swim through coral reefs. "This reef is struggling," the globe said gently. "Warmer water makes it harder for coral to survive." "What can I do?" Loopy asked. "Start small. Use less plastic. Tell your friends. And keep learning." Loopy nodded. He didn't have all the answers, but now he understood the question. And that was the beginning.

Games Room

Play With a Purpose

The Games Room is where fun meets learning. It's a collection of short, engaging mini-games that teach sustainability concepts through play. Think sorting waste, matching animals to habitats, water-saving challenges, and seasonal nature quizzes. Games are designed for different age groups, quick to play, and connected to real missions. The Games Room makes sustainability something kids actually want to do, and parents can join in too.

Why It Matters

Play is how children learn best. Short, purposeful games reinforce what kids learn through missions and make the platform a place they want to come back to.

Loopy heard laughter coming from the hollow log. Inside, Mira the fox was sorting acorns and pinecones into baskets. "What are you doing?" Loopy asked. "Playing the Sorting Game!" Mira said. "You have to put each thing in the right place: compost, recycle, or reuse. Want to try?" Loopy tried. He got a few wrong at first, but then he got faster. "I didn't know banana peels could be composted!" he said. "Now you do," Mira grinned. "And tomorrow, you'll remember." They played three more rounds. Learning had never felt this fun.

Activity Hut

Hands-On Projects for All Ages

The Activity Hut is EarthPatchers' creative workshop. It's a space full of DIY activities, nature crafts, seasonal projects, and hands-on guides that families and young people can do together. Think birdhouse building, seed bombing, DIY beeswax wraps, upcycling old t-shirts, and seasonal nature journals. Every activity is designed to be fun, educational, and connected to real sustainability goals. Activities are tagged by age, difficulty, season, and materials needed, so it's easy to find something that works for you.

Why It Matters

Doing things with your hands builds real understanding. The Activity Hut turns sustainability into something tangible, shareable, and genuinely fun for the whole family.

Loopy walked into the Activity Hut and gasped. The walls were covered in drawings, dried flowers, and tiny birdhouses. "What's today's activity?" he asked. "Seed bombs!" said Lina the rabbit, holding up a ball of mud and seeds. "You roll them, dry them, and throw them in a field. Flowers grow where they land." Loopy made seven seed bombs. Some were lumpy, some were perfect. He carried them to the edge of the meadow and tossed them one by one. "By spring," Lina said, "this whole field will be full of wildflowers." Loopy couldn't wait.

Eco Shops Directory

Find What's Truly Sustainable

The Eco Shops Directory is a curated, community-driven guide to sustainable shops and services in your city. Starting in Cluj, it helps people find places that genuinely care about the environment, with clear criteria: refill stations, compostable products, recycled or reclaimed materials, repair services. Community members can contribute new listings using a simple template (photos, location, what makes it sustainable, pros and cons). Over time, the directory expands city by city, driven by demand and community voting.

Why It Matters

Finding truly sustainable shops is hard. This directory makes it simple, with clear criteria and community-driven reviews, so people can make better choices without hours of research.

Loopy needed a new notebook, but he didn't want one wrapped in plastic. "Try the Eco Shops board," Mira suggested. The board was pinned to the big oak tree: a map of the forest with little green flags. "Green Acorn Stationery," Loopy read. "Recycled paper, no plastic, refills available." He followed the trail to a small shop run by a family of squirrels. The notebook was beautiful: thick recycled pages with a cover made from pressed leaves. "Who added this to the board?" Loopy asked. "I did," said Pip the hedgehog proudly. "I found this place last month." That's how the directory grew: one good find at a time.

The Green Wall

Where Ideas Become Real

The Green Wall is EarthPatchers' community idea board. It's where anyone can post an idea for a sustainability project, and the community votes on what matters most (Impact + Feasibility). People join ideas with their skills (design, education, tech, legal, organising), and ideas rise as teams form and support appears. It's like a public startup board for the community: transparent, skill-driven, and focused on turning good intentions into real projects. Ideas that gain traction can attract mentors, sponsors, or local funding.

Why It Matters

Good ideas need more than enthusiasm. They need skills, teamwork, and sometimes funding. The Green Wall turns community energy into real projects.

Loopy had an idea: "What if we planted fruit trees along the main path so everyone could eat for free in autumn?" He wrote it on a leaf and pinned it to the Green Wall. The next day, three animals had voted for it. By the end of the week, a whole team had formed: Barbu knew where to plant, Mira knew which trees grew best, and Pip offered to water them every morning. "All it took," Loopy thought, "was one leaf on a wall." By autumn, the path was lined with apple and plum trees. And every animal who walked by took a bite and smiled.

Organisations & Scientists

Trusted Knowledge for Real Action

This module connects EarthPatchers to the world of trusted science, research, and expert organisations. It's a curated space where verified scientists, environmental NGOs, and research teams can share knowledge, co-create missions, and provide fact-checked content for the platform's learning layer. Community members can see who stands behind the information they're learning, making trust visible. Over time, it becomes a bridge between grassroots community action and the research world, ensuring that what people do is backed by real evidence.

Why It Matters

Sustainability needs trusted information. This module ensures that the platform's content is reliable, evidence-based, and connected to real experts who care about making science accessible.

Loopy had a question nobody in the forest could answer: "Why is the stream warmer this year?" He followed the signs to the big oak where Professor Hoot the owl held office hours. "Ah," said Professor Hoot, adjusting her glasses. "That's a very good question. Let me show you the data." She unrolled a long scroll with charts and pictures. "The stream is warmer because the trees upstream were cut down. Without shade, the water heats up." "Can we fix it?" Loopy asked. "Yes. Plant shade trees along the bank. I'll show your tribe exactly where and what to plant." Loopy smiled. Now they didn't just have a feeling. They had a plan.

New Cities & Discovery Phase

Growing Together, City by City

New Cities & Discovery Phase is how EarthPatchers grows beyond Cluj. It's a transparent, community-driven expansion model where the community votes on the next city to join, and resources are managed through clear "City Funds" that show exactly what gets delivered at 30%, 60%, and 100% funding. Every new city starts with a Discovery Phase: a light version of the platform where early adopters test missions, build a local tribe, and help shape the city's unique character on the platform. It's not just about copying, it's about growing together.

Why It Matters

Scaling should be transparent and community-driven. City Funds let everyone see where resources go, and voting ensures expansion reflects real demand.

One day, a letter arrived at the forest clearing. "Dear EarthPatchers," it read. "We're a group of animals from the Mountain Valley. We heard about what you're doing and we want to start something similar here." Loopy gathered the tribe. "They want to join," he said. "But they need help getting started." "We could send them our mission cards," suggested Mira. "And our rulebook," added Barbu. "And we'll visit once to help them set up their own Green Wall." A few weeks later, the Mountain Valley had its own clearing, its own tribe, and its own missions. Different forest, same spirit.

Patcher Camp

The Big Adventure Game

Patcher Camp is a cooperative adventure game set across four climate zones, where young people solve real environmental problems using their skills as superpowers. The four elements (Earth, Water, Fire, Wind) represent different sustainability themes: materials, resources, energy, and mobility. Players work together to restore balance, complete real-life micro-tasks (cleaning, repair, cooking, upcycling), and unlock new abilities. There's also a Wizard Tent, a calm, reflective space with zero-waste recipes and debrief activities, designed especially for older teens (14-18).

Why It Matters

Games make learning irresistible. Patcher Camp connects play to real-world action, helping young people see their skills as superpowers for the planet.

Loopy put on his adventure bandana and stepped into Patcher Camp. The forest around him shimmered. "Choose your element," the old tree spirit said. "Earth," Loopy said. "I want to help with materials." His badge glowed green. His first quest: find three things in the forest that could be reused instead of thrown away. He found an old jar (lantern!), a broken branch (walking stick!), and a torn net (plant holder!). "Three out of three," the tree spirit smiled. "You've levelled up, Earth Patcher." Loopy grinned. He wasn't just playing a game. He was becoming someone who could fix the world, one small thing at a time.

About Us

Our Mission

To make sustainable living feel doable, social, and rewarding by giving people clear actions (missions), a safe community to learn together, and practical ways to reuse instead of waste.

Our Vision

A world where doing good for the planet is as normal as brushing your teeth: simple habits, local support, and young people growing up with the skills and confidence to protect their future.

Over time, EarthPatchers can expand city-by-city into a network of communities that take measurable local action, learn why it matters in an age-appropriate way, and make circular living accessible to everyone.

Why EarthPatchers

The missing piece isn't more awareness. It's an easy path from intention to action, with support and trust built in. EarthPatchers bridges the gap between wanting to help and knowing how.
