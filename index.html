    Patching the Earth Together, One Stitch at a Time

    Small, clear missions you can do without an internet scavenger hunt.
    We repair the planet too, but we start with what matters most to you: time, budget, and daily chaos.

    No superheroes. No drama. Just small steps, done together.

    The Problem We Solve

    We are not short on information.
    We are short on time, clarity, and simple ways to act.

    The ideas that help you save money, waste less, or do something useful are scattered everywhere — across websites, reports, and social media — like socks after laundry day: everywhere, when all you want is to have them neatly in a drawer, where they belong.

    EarthPatchers gathers them in one place.
    Not something to research.
    Something to do.

    Mission Examples

    A mission is a practical action you can complete in real life that helps your neighborhood, your community, and the natural world.

    Important!

    Some features are still being built.

    For now:

    • Missions are validated by leaders (MasterPatchers) or by sending proof by email
    • Patch Coins are tracked as a visible thank-you for real effort.

    As the platform grows, validation and rewards will become fully integrated.

    Examples by mission size and age group:

    Small Mission (10 Patch Coins)

    • Durată: aproximativ 1–20 de minute, o singură dată sau repetată pe parcursul câtorva zile, până devine un obicei.
    • Copii: udă un copac sau o plantă din apropiere și urmărește-o timp de o săptămână.
    • Adolescenți: organizează, timp de o săptămână, o provocare în familie pentru a economisi apă acasă.
    • Adulți: verifică frigiderul, găsește 2 lucruri care trebuie consumate azi sau mâine și mută-le în față.
    See Small Missions

    Medium Mission (25 Patch Coins)

    • Time: about 45–60 minutes, usually completed in one session over the course of a week.
    • Kids + family: park or riverbank clean-up.
    • Teens: take one no-car day and use bike, bus, or walking.
    • Adults: donate one item you no longer use (for example a pair of sunglasses, a book, or a jacket).
    See Medium Missions

    Big Mission (50 Patch Coins)

    • Time: about half a day to a full day, usually during the weekend.
    • Kids + school: build and maintain a pollinator corner in the school yard.
    • Teens: lead a neighborhood energy-saving mini campaign with measurable results.
    • Adults: coordinate a community day with clean-up, repair, and tree-care actions.
    See Large Missions
    Then you choose how:
    • At home: small, quick missions for busy days.
    • In a Tribe: build your own team or join one already started by people like you.
    • With NGOs: join a trusted NGO’s Tribe and help with their missions.

    No stress. No pressure.
    Just practical action, shared effort, and a reminder that big things rarely get fixed alone.

    A Tribe is a small team.

    The kind of team where you know who brings snacks and who always forgets socks.
    Like a camp club, but for real life.

    • adults-only

    36-Month Timeline

    Phase 1 · Months 0–12

    Prove the Core Loop in Early Pilots

    • Launch the missions library and start tracking progress
    • Run the first youth community pilot groups with strong safety rules
    • Launch the Market Hut pilot, with moderation and clear guidance for safe pickup
    • Add the first "learning corner" (small, curated, age-aware)
    Goal

    Prove people return, participate, and feel safe.

    Phase 2 · Months 13–24

    Make It Stronger & Easier to Run

    • Improve mission quality and "mission packs" (seasonal collections)
    • Expand youth pilot groups if Phase 1 works well
    • Improve safe exchange flows (categories, templates, trust signals)
    • Grow the learning layer carefully (still staged)
    Goal

    Reduce admin/moderation effort while improving experience and safety.

    Phase 3 · Months 25–36

    Make It Stable & Scale-Ready

    • Make the three pillars robust enough for growth
    • Ensure safety workflows work at larger scale
    • Strengthen the learning layer into clear learning paths connected to missions
    Goal

    EarthPatchers becomes repeatable as a model that can adapt to different locations.

    What Success Looks Like

    • Missions will be completed and people come back because missions feel doable and worth it.
    • Parents feel comfortable letting kids participate — and kids actually enjoy coming back.
    • Circular exchange works smoothly with trust and moderation
    • The staged learning layer helps people understand "why" and "what to do next"
    • Pilots shape how we grow, based on what works and what doesn’t, not assumptions.

    Base Camp

    Missions HQ

    Missions HQ is where EarthPatchers begins. It is a library of real-life missions for kids, teens, and adults — organised by time, type, and group. Missions come in sizes that fit busy life: • Tiny missions (10–20 minutes) • Weekend missions (1–2 hours) • Team missions (families, schools, clubs, Tribes) Adults can filter missions by time, topic, and skills (repairing, organising, teaching, gardening, design, photography), so helping can match what people are actually good at. For now, missions can be validated by leaders (MasterPatchers) or by sending the requested proof to us by email until website mission validation is ready.

    Rewards stay simple:

    Patch Coins: you earn them through missions and learning, and they later have practical use in modules like Market Hut and Games Room.

    Badges: they reflect the kind of person you are becoming (brave, kind, curious).

    Achievements: they capture what you did along the way, like stickers in your adventure notebook.

    Why It Matters

    Missions turn caring for the planet into an adventure. They give people a clear starting point: one mission at a time, building real change step by step.

    Tribes

    Tribes are small groups where people take action together.

    • For kids and teens, with adult leaders
      • Kids use aliases and avatars, with no visible faces
      • Activity stays within the Tribe
    • Adults-only
      • They decide how visible public their involvement is

    Tribe leaders have verified accounts. They help with moderation, keep things safe and respectful, and support the group.

    Why It Matters

    Tribes aren’t just about tasks — they’re about belonging. They help turn the platform into a community where people support each other, act together, and keep things safe and kind.

    Base Camp

    Market Hut

    The Market Hut is a circular exchange where people reuse items instead of buying new. Patch Coins make it work without awkward item-for-item matching: you earn coins by helping, then use coins to choose items. Safety guidance is built into the flow. Adults choose their visibility, but children’s faces are never allowed.

    Why It Matters

    Circular habits are one of the fastest ways to reduce waste. Patch Coins make reuse simple and fair — no awkward item-for-item matching, just kindness and coins.

    Coming Later

    • Optional polite bidding using Patch Coins for high-demand items (simple and kid-safe).
    • Borrowing shelves (tools, books, sports gear, kids gear).
    • Better trust signals (verified users and mission-verified trading privileges).
    • Safer meetup and pickup guidance with clearer rules.

    Earth's Side of the Story

    Earth’s Side of the Story is the learning-and-action world that connects learning to missions. It has three doors: 1) My Hazards • Explains hazards in a friendly way: - natural hazards - natural hazards made worse by pollution/changes people cause - hazards caused directly by people • Short learning cards and quizzes earn Patch Coins. 2) Contamination & Recovery Zone (local map where relevant) Adults (and older teens where appropriate) can add a pin (photo + short note) for: • littering hot spots • damaged areas / broken things • trees cut where they shouldn’t be • places needing care Pins can become missions (or requests for missions). Later, this map can include verified helper pins: • Helpers (NGOs, researchers, community groups) • Missions (ways to help) • Reports (things needing attention) 3) Learn & Play Short learning paths and quizzes that connect back to missions and Patch Coins.

    Why It Matters

    Understanding "why" is the bridge between action and lasting change. This module makes learning accessible, emotionally safe, and directly connected to real missions.

    Coming Later

    • Tribe alerts: new pins can gently alert nearby Tribes so local teams can choose to help.
    • New cities/countries open slowly via a Discovery Phase (data + partners + priority missions). If discovery isn't supported, the area stays "sleeping" until the community helps find data sources or partners.
    • Community help for discovery (finding data sources and mapping local helpers).
    • Verified helper pins (NGOs, scientists, community groups) and mission pins.
    • Better learning paths (age-appropriate, including adult paths).

    Activity Hut

    The Activity Hut is a personal home base that feels like an adventure journal. It shows: • your adventure trail (missions and milestones) • Patch Coins now (spendable) and Patch Coins earned (lifetime effort) • badges and achievements • role path (like Leader, or MasterPatcher as we call it) Adults choose what to share publicly; kids stay within Tribe visibility rules.

    Future Module

    Eco Shops Directory

    Eco Shops is a guide to sustainable products and local shops with ratings and comments to keep things honest. Quality rules reduce spam: • You can review or add products after earning enough Patch Coins and completing a verified mission. • New products are submitted via a template (photo, where to buy, materials, why it’s better, what to watch out for). • Leaders, also called MasterPatchers, verify entries before they go live.

    Why It Matters

    Finding truly sustainable products is hard. This directory keeps things honest with community reviews, verified missions, and Leader oversight (MasterPatcher role).

    Who We Are

    Our Mission

    When something feels useful and worth your time in real life, it’s easier to give it a try. That’s why we offer a platform with clear missions you can actually follow in real life, a safe community, and simple reuse solutions.

    And along the way, we repair the planet too — one mission at a time.
    With superheroes, but without all the drama. Just small steps, done together.

    Our Vision

    A world where reusing and repairing are as routine as brushing your teeth.

    Why EarthPatchers

    Because you don’t have time to figure everything out.

    Because small things pile up — things to fix, reuse, or stop wasting.

    Because life is already full, and “I’ll deal with it later” quietly becomes “not today again.”

    EarthPatchers turns those moments into clear, doable steps.

    No searching. No overwhelm. Just a place to start.

    So we focus on one simple question: not “How do you do everything?” but “Where do you start?”

    Our Values

    Sustainability

    Real action over awareness campaigns. Small steps that add up to lasting change.

    Community

    Local support, shared missions, and the power of acting together.

    Transparency

    Clear budgets. Public plans. Donations explained.
    You know what you support. You know where it goes.

    Child Safety

    Privacy-by-design, age-appropriate content, and guardian involvement at every step.
