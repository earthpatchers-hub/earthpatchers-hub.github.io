EarthPatchers

Community-Powered Sustainability

EarthPatchers is a magical camp-style community where kids and adults complete real missions to save the Earth, learn how the world works, and reuse things instead of wasting them.

Why EarthPatchers Exists

Because the world's problems feel enormous, and people freeze. EarthPatchers turns “too big” into small brave steps that fit real life, then connects those steps to a local community (starting in Cluj).

The missing piece isn't more awareness. It's a clear, easy path from intention to action, with built-in trust, support, and a community that moves together.

MVP — 36-Month Plan

EarthPatchers is starting with a focused MVP that proves the platform works in real life, in Cluj, before we expand. This MVP has three pillars:

🎯

Missions

Real-world eco actions

Quick missions (10–20 min) and weekend missions (1–2 hours), filtered by age, interest, and time. Progress tracked with badges, points, and a personal timeline.

Missions turn climate anxiety into doable action and give families and youth a clear starting point.

🤝

Youth Community Pilot

Small, safe, meaningful

Small pilot groups in Cluj with a realistic rhythm: monthly or seasonal challenges. Clear rules, reporting, and adult/guardian involvement where needed.

Young people care deeply about their future. This gives them a safe way to contribute without overwhelm.

♻️

Safe Circular Exchange

Swap, give away, reuse

Local swap and give-away listings for Cluj. Safety-by-design: clear exchange rules, safe pickup practices, and moderation for suspicious behavior.

Circular habits are one of the fastest ways to reduce waste, but only work if people trust the system.

36-Month Timeline

Phase 1 · Months 0–12

Prove the Core Loop in Cluj

  • Launch the missions library and start tracking progress
  • Run the first youth community pilot groups with strong safety rules
  • Launch the circular exchange pilot with moderation and safe pickup guidance
  • Add the first "learning corner" (small, curated, age-aware)
Goal

Prove people return, participate, and feel safe.

Phase 2 · Months 13–24

Make It Stronger & Easier to Run

  • Improve mission quality and "mission packs" (seasonal collections)
  • Expand youth pilot groups if Phase 1 works well
  • Improve safe exchange flows (categories, templates, trust signals)
  • Grow the learning layer carefully (still staged)
Goal

Reduce admin/moderation effort while improving experience and safety.

Phase 3 · Months 25–36

Make It Stable & Scale-Ready

  • Make the three pillars robust enough for growth
  • Ensure safety workflows work at larger scale
  • Strengthen the learning layer into clear learning paths connected to missions
Goal

EarthPatchers becomes repeatable as a model, starting from Cluj.

The "Globe" Is Staged

Our future learning experience (an "Encarta-like" Earth globe/map) is added step-by-step as a learning layer that supports Missions:

  • Phase 1: A small learning corner (simple, clear, age-aware)
  • Phase 2: A richer learning map with carefully chosen topics
  • Phase 3: More complete learning paths (still focused on clarity and reliability)

We want learning to be trustworthy and understandable, not flashy and confusing.

What the MVP Does NOT Include

EarthPatchers has many future ideas (eco shops directory, idea backlog, games, city expansion tools). These are not part of the MVP and will be developed as Next Steps, so people don't confuse the MVP with everything we might build.

What Success Looks Like

  • ✅ Missions are completed and people return for more
  • 🔐 Youth participation is safe, supportive, and meaningful
  • ♻️ Circular exchange works smoothly with trust and moderation
  • 📚 The staged learning layer helps people understand "why" and "what to do next"
  • 🏙️ Cluj becomes a strong foundation for future expansion

Missions HQ

Where It All Begins

Missions HQ is where EarthPatchers begins. It’s a library of real-life missions for kids, teens, and adults, designed to make “saving the Earth” feel doable. Missions come in sizes that fit busy life: • Tiny missions (10–20 minutes) • Weekend missions (1–2 hours) • Team missions (families, schools, clubs, Tribes) Adults can filter missions by time, topic, and skills (repairing, organising, teaching, gardening, design, photography), so helping can match what people are actually good at. Rewards stay simple: • Patch Coins are earned through missions and learning and can be used in the Market Hut (and later Games). • Badges are about who you are becoming (brave, kind, curious). • Achievements are things you did, like stickers in your book.

Why It Matters

Missions turn climate anxiety into doable action. They give families and young people a clear starting point: one mission at a time, building real change step by step.

Once upon a time there was a bear named Loopy.
We shall call him Loopy the Bear, because well… he insisted.

Loopy wore a hat with a leaf on it. It was far too fancy for a bear.
But Loopy was a fancy bear, and that was that.

One morning, Loopy wandered into a clearing and found a wooden sign:

MISSIONS HQ

“Hm,” Loopy whispered, “Headquarters. That sounds like a place where serious things happen… like secret plans… and maybe biscuits.”

Around a warm story-fire sat little people and big people. They held mission cards like treasure maps.

“…to save the Earth,” someone said.

Loopy froze mid-tiptoe. “Save the Earth? That is a very big sentence.”

A child lifted a card: “Mission: Lights Ninja!”
The dad smiled. “If you catch the lights leaving rooms empty, you win.”
The child clicked off a switch and returned like a tiny hero.

The dad tried not to look too excited. “The bill might be smaller this month.”
Loopy thought, Humans pretend not to enjoy saving money… but their eyebrows tell the truth.

Another card appeared: “Mission: Fix One Thing.”
Loopy gasped (he always did). “Fixing things is like telling the planet: ‘You don’t have to make a new one. We can be clever.’”

When the mission ended, a badge floated down like a friendly firefly: Kind. Brave. Curious.
Then a banner popped up: Achievement Unlocked: First Mission Completed.

Loopy found a sign that explained it simply:
Badges are about who you are becoming.
Achievements are about things you did, like stickers in your book.

Then Patch Coins twinkled in like pocket stars.

“This is not just a plan,” Loopy said softly.
“This is a loop.”

Tribes

Your Safe Team Space

A Tribe is EarthPatchers’ word for a small team (like a club). Tribes can be: • Kids/Teens Tribes (aliases + avatars, no visible kids’ faces, kid activity visible only in the Tribe) • Adults-only Tribes (adults choose their visibility) Missions can be shared across Tribes so great missions spread without exposing children’s accounts. Tribe “camp rules” keep things kind and clean: • Be kind • Keep private details private • No visible kids’ faces • Don’t post littering (and don’t encourage it) • Use a Littering report when something shouldn’t be there

Why It Matters

Sustainability isn’t just about tasks — it’s about belonging. Tribes turn the platform into a community where people support each other, act together, and keep things safe and kind.

The next day Loopy returned and found another sign, slightly crooked, as if a squirrel had installed it while running:

TRIBES

Loopy squinted. “Tribes… that sounds like a place where teams keep important secrets… and maybe snacks.”
(He hoped for snacks. Loopy always hoped for snacks.)

Inside, lanterns hung like little moons. It felt like a clubhouse built out of kindness.
A leader held a wooden board covered in stickers. Loopy approved immediately. Stickers meant someone cared.

Two painted doors appeared:
Kids & Teens Tribe
Adults Tribe

Behind the kids’ door were superhero names and avatars. No face photos. A safe storybook world.
Behind the adults’ door, grown-ups chose their own style: some real names, some aliases, some photos, some none.

A “Mission Shelf” stood in the middle. Missions could be placed there so other Tribes could borrow them like recipes.

Loopy nodded. “If one team makes an amazing mission, other teams can do it too.”

Then Loopy saw the rules lantern:
Be kind.
Keep private details private.
No visible kids’ faces.
Don’t post littering.
If something yucky appears, use the Littering button.

“These rules don’t steal the magic,” Loopy whispered.
“They guard it.”

Market Hut

Swap, Share, Reuse

The Market Hut is a Cluj-first circular exchange where people reuse items instead of buying new. Patch Coins make it work without awkward item-for-item matching: you earn coins by helping, then use coins to choose items. Safety guidance is built into the flow. Adults choose their visibility, but children’s faces are never allowed.

Why It Matters

Circular habits are one of the fastest ways to reduce waste. Patch Coins make reuse simple and fair — no awkward item-for-item matching, just kindness and coins.

Loopy followed a scent trail that smelled like “still useful.”
He found a little hut full of treasures: jackets, books, toys, puzzles with proud eyes.

A tag said: 120 Patch Coins.

Patch Coins are thank-you coins, the sign explained.
You earn them by saving the Earth in small ways.
You use them to choose treasures other people don’t need anymore.

Loopy finally understood the secret:
Swapping item-for-item is tricky.
But coins make it fair and simple.
You don’t match jacket-to-puzzle perfectly.
You match kindness… and the coins do the rest.

Loopy left the hut smiling, because the Market Hut wasn’t about stuff.
It was about second chances.

Earth's Side of the Story

Learning That Starts in Cluj

Earth’s Side of the Story is the learning-and-action world that starts in Cluj and connects learning to missions. It has three doors: 1) My Hazards • Explains hazards in a friendly way: - natural hazards - natural hazards made worse by pollution/changes people cause - hazards caused directly by people • Short learning cards and quizzes earn Patch Coins. 2) Contamination & Recovery Zone (Cluj map) Adults (and older teens where appropriate) can add a pin (photo + short note) for: • littering hot spots • damaged areas / broken things • trees cut where they shouldn’t be • places needing care Pins can become missions (or requests for missions). Later, this map can include verified helper pins: • Helpers (NGOs, researchers, community groups) • Missions (ways to help) • Reports (things needing attention) 3) Learn & Play Short learning paths and quizzes that connect back to missions and Patch Coins. Next steps (later): • Tribe alerts: new pins can gently alert nearby Tribes so local teams can choose to help. • New cities/countries open slowly via a Discovery Phase (data + partners + priority missions). If discovery isn’t supported, the area stays “sleeping” until the community helps find data sources or partners.

Why It Matters

Understanding “why” is the bridge between action and lasting change. This module makes learning accessible, emotionally safe, and directly connected to real missions.

Loopy returned to the biggest storybook he had ever seen:

THE EARTH’S SIDE OF THE STORY

“Earth,” Loopy whispered, “please explain yourself in a way a bear can understand.”

The book opened with a soft whoosh. Inside were three adventure doors.

Door One: My Hazards
A shelf held three jars:
Nature.
Nature, made worse.
People-made.

A kind sentence appeared:
Sometimes nature is wild.
Sometimes it gets worse when people change the air, the water, or the land.
Sometimes people accidentally build problems… then learn how to build better.

Loopy nodded. “Clear. Like bedtime.”

He tried a quiz. Patch Coins twinkled into his pocket like pocket stars.
“Learning that gives you coins,” Loopy said. “Finally.”

Door Two: Contamination & Recovery Zone
A map opened, glowing with one warm place:

Cluj.

Adults placed pins with photos and tiny notes:
“This spot has littering.”
“This bench is broken.”
“This tree was cut and it doesn’t look right.”

Loopy whispered, “That’s very useful pointing.”

Some pins could become missions.
“So learning doesn’t float away,” Loopy smiled. “It turns into action.”

Later, the map could show helper pins too: teams who clean rivers, study air, restore forests.
“A map that shows helpers,” Loopy whispered, “is a map with hope.”

And later still, new pins could give nearby Tribes a gentle lantern-nudge:
Something here needs care.
Not loud. Just an invitation to be brave and helpful.

Door Three: Learn & Play
The last room felt like puzzles, not homework. Loopy nearly answered like a bear (more berries).
The book offered another card, kindly.

At the edge of the page, other cities were faint outlines, sleeping chapters.
Before they open, the book said, we do a Discovery Phase: data, partners, priority missions.
Loopy smiled. “Good. No half-built chapters.”

Games Room

Play With a Purpose

The Games Room starts by embedding trusted nature-learning games so EarthPatchers can offer play-based learning quickly. Patch Coins unlock games (simple rule: coins open doors). Adults can play too and earn Patch Coins. Later: EarthPatchers-made games, and eventually Patcher Camp.

Why It Matters

Play is how children learn best. Starting with trusted games means learning happens fast, and Patch Coins keep engagement connected to real effort.

Loopy found a door that hummed with quiet giggles:

GAMES ROOM

Inside wasn’t just one game. It was a room where many games could visit.
Some games were trusted guests that taught nature without feeling like homework.

Loopy nodded. “We invite good games to the party first… and later we bake our own cake.”

A small chest had a lock labeled Patch Coins.
Loopy grinned. “Coins open doors. Simple.”

Activity Hut

Your Adventure Journal

The Activity Hut is a personal home base that feels like an adventure journal. It shows: • your adventure trail (missions and milestones) • Patch Coins now (spendable) and Patch Coins earned (lifetime effort) • badges and achievements • role path (like MasterPatcher) Adults choose what to share publicly; kids stay within Tribe visibility rules. Next steps (later): • Safe kid-friendly AI can create a child’s Hero Story: a bedtime-story retelling of verified adventures, using alias + avatar only.

Why It Matters

Your adventure journal is where effort becomes visible. Badges show who you’re becoming, achievements show what you’ve done, and your Patch Coins total shows your lifetime impact.

Eco Shops Directory

Find What’s Truly Sustainable

Eco Shops is a Cluj-first guide to sustainable products and local shops with ratings and comments to keep things honest. Quality rules reduce spam: • You can review or add products after earning enough Patch Coins and completing a verified mission. • New products are submitted via a template (photo, where to buy, materials, why it’s better, what to watch out for). • MasterPatchers verify entries before they go live.

Why It Matters

Finding truly sustainable products is hard. This directory keeps things honest with community reviews, verified missions, and MasterPatcher oversight.

Loopy opened a guidebook full of notes and tiny opinions:
“This used to be refill.”
“This packaging changed.”
“This one is genuinely compostable.”

Loopy nodded. “Honesty is brave.”

A friendly rule glowed:
“You can only review orate or add products after you’ve earned Patch Coins and completed a verified mission.”

Loopy smiled. “So you don’t just wander in and shout opinions. You become a helper first.”

The Green Wall

Where Ideas Grow

The Green Wall is an idea garden where people post ideas, vote, and form teams based on skills. Ideas can be: • local community projects (repair clubs, refill stations) • next steps for EarthPatchers itself (new features, new modules, expansion ideas) Helper Page explanation: The Helper Page is found inside the Activity Hut. It’s the part of your profile that can show what you learned and how you helped (optional sharing, adult-controlled).

Why It Matters

Good ideas need more than enthusiasm — they need skills, teamwork, and sometimes funding. The Green Wall turns community energy into real projects.

Loopy found a wall covered in idea-leaves.
Some ideas were for the outside world. Some were for EarthPatchers itself.

People joined ideas like puzzle pieces:
“I can design.”
“I can organise.”
“I can code.”
“I can teach.”

Loopy grinned and announced:
“This wall doesn’t keep you out.
It grows branches over your head like a big tree crown… so even your ideas can sit in the shade and think.”

Organisations & Scientists

Helper Pins on the Map

Verified organisations and researchers appear as special Helper pins within the Contamination & Recovery Zone in Earth’s Side of the Story. Pin types can include: • Helpers (NGOs, researchers, community groups) • Missions (ways to help) • Reports (things needing attention) This helps people discover real helper teams, learn what they do in clear language, and join missions.

Why It Matters

Seeing who helps — and how to join them — turns a map of problems into a map of hope. Verified helper pins connect community action to real expertise.

Loopy opened the map again and saw helper pins.
“This isn’t just ‘what’s wrong’,” he whispered.
“It’s also ‘who’s helping’ and ‘how to join.’”

Loopy nodded. “A map that shows helpers is a map with hope.”

Patcher Camp

The Big Adventure Game

Patcher Camp is a future flagship teamwork game. Kids work together through Earth zones using “powers” that are real knowledge: Earth, Water, Fire, Wind. It teaches practical skills, problem-solving, and brave teamwork. It comes after the Games Room foundation is working.

Games make learning irresistible. Patcher Camp connects play to real-world action, helping young people see their knowledge as superpowers for the planet.

Loopy found a rolled-up poster labeled PATCHER CAMP.

Loopy whispered, “Their superpower is learning.”

He tied the poster closed gently.
“Not yet,” he smiled. “First we open the Games Room properly. Then we build our big adventure.”

About Us

Our Mission

To make sustainable living feel doable, social, and rewarding by giving people clear actions (missions), a safe community to learn together, and practical ways to reuse instead of waste.

Our Vision

A world where doing good for the planet is as normal as brushing your teeth: simple habits, local support, and young people growing up with the skills and confidence to protect their future.

Over time, EarthPatchers can expand city-by-city into a network of communities that take measurable local action, learn why it matters in an age-appropriate way, and make circular living accessible to everyone.

Why EarthPatchers

The missing piece isn't more awareness. It's an easy path from intention to action, with support and trust built in. EarthPatchers bridges the gap between wanting to help and knowing how.

Our Values

🌱

Sustainability

Real action over awareness campaigns. Small steps that add up to lasting change.

🤝

Community

Local support, shared missions, and the power of acting together.

🔎

Transparency

Open plans, community voting, and clear funding accountability.

🔐

Child Safety

Privacy-by-design, age-appropriate content, and guardian involvement at every step.

How We Work

We work through partnerships with schools and NGOs, pilot programs in Cluj, and community voting to decide what gets built next. Everything starts small, gets tested, and grows based on real feedback.
